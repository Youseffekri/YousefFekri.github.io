Yousef Fekri's Homepage

I am a Ph.D. student in Computer Science at The University of Georgia, working under the supervision of Dr. John A. Miller. My research focuses on neural symbolic regression for multivariate time series forecasting, aiming to develop interpretable models that maintain high predictive accuracy while reducing computational complexity. While deep learning methods achieve strong predictive performance, their black-box nature limits interpretability, making them less suitable for scientific discovery. To address this, I have developed ARX_Symb, a lightweight neural symbolic regression framework that enhances forecasting by integrating stepwise feature selection and attention-based feature importance. By refining symbolic equations to extract meaningful patterns, my approach balances accuracy and interpretability while improving computational efficiency. We plan to improve attention-based selection by incorporating a pre-training phase and also apply nonlinear models to find the best exponents for the endogenous and exogenous variables in our model, further enhancing its ability to identify meaningful patterns in time series data.

Background

I have a strong mathematical background, earning an M.Sc. in applied mathematics from Sharif University of Technology and a B.Sc. in pure mathematics from Semnan University. This foundation has allowed me to approach machine learning problems with both theoretical rigor and practical implementation skills. In addition to my research in neural symbolic regression, I have worked on implementing and benchmarking machine learning models in Scala, including AR, ARX, ARIMA, NN, CNN, and Transformers. My projects also extend to LLMs for humor and sarcasm detection, as well as deep learning-based fake image region detection. These diverse experiences have strengthened my ability to develop efficient, scalable, and interpretable AI models across different domains.
